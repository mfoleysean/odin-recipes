Old Fashioned Cocktail

Old Fashioned

A versatile, classic cocktail

The Old Fashioned is a simple mixed drink built upon the minimum requirements of a cocktail dating back to the early 1800s: spirits, bitters, sugar, and water.

The simplicity of the Old Fashioned enables it to be customized in many ways from using either bourbon or rye whiskeys, or other spirits like rum and brandy. You can also experiment with different bitters, simple syrups, sugar cubes, or maple syrup. You can also try different garnishes for a unique twist such as mulled cherries, cinnamon, vanilla, a mulled orange slice, or an expressed orange peel.

The following recipe uses bourbon, Angostura bitters, a sugar cube, water, and an orange peel garnish to provide a good starting point before you begin experimenting with your own style of the Old Fashioned.

Ingredients

  • 1 rocks/lowball glass
  • 2 to 3 ice cubes
  • 1 sugar cube
  • 1 teaspoon of water
  • 3 dashes of Angostura bitters
  • 2 ounces of bourbon
  • 1 orange peel garnish (about the width and length of your thumb)

Recipe Directions

  1. Place the sugar cube in the glass, then add bitters and water
  2. Stir or muddle the mixture until the sugar is dissolved
  3. Place your ice cube into the mixture then add bourbon
  4. Gently stir to combine
  5. Express the orange peel garnish over the glass by twisting it gently, then drop it in the glass

As mentioned above, the Old Fashioned is a cocktail that lends itself to creativity. Try different combinations of spirits, bitters, sugars, and garnishes to create your own personal style of this classic!
